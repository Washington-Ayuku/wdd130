Rafting and whitewater rafting are recreational outdoor activities which use an inflatable raft to navigate a river or other body of water. This is often done on whitewater or different degrees of rough water. Dealing with risk is often a part of the experience.

Overview

The act, sport, or pastime of traveling on a river or other body of water in a usually inflatable raft. and basely this activity is carried out during vacations and holidays and more esspecialy on summertimes for the American continents and europeans. There are three main types of rafts: paddle boats, gear boats and oar/paddle combination rafts. Each craft has its specific uses. Paddle boats can carry the most people, require the least amount of rigging. In general, white water rafting can reach speeds of 10-20 miles per hour in turbulent sections of the river. However, it's important to note that safety should always be the top priority when engaging in this adventurous activity. Rivers increase speed at a narrow.
